02 / 15 · The Problem
The problem

Too many tools.
Too much admin.

Most nutritionists manage client data across messages, spreadsheets, PDFs, calendars, recipes, and notes. The result: duplicated work, lost context, and less time for real client support.

Manual body-composition data entry

Typing numbers from InBody, SECA, or body-composition reports into spreadsheets takes time and creates errors.

Scattered client communication

Client messages live across WhatsApp, email, SMS, and calls. Nothing is centralized or easy to track.

Slow diet-plan creation

Plans are often rebuilt in Word, Excel, PDFs, or old templates instead of reusable structured workflows.

No single source of truth

Client notes, goals, progress, appointments, and reports are spread across different tools.

Recipe & food data chaos

Recipes, macros, custom foods, and meal ideas are hard to reuse when they are saved in different places.

Missed follow-ups

Without reminders, progress alerts, and clear client history, important check-ins can easily slip through.
